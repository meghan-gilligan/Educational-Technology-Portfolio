01 — About

A practitioner who
designs for the person
in front of them

My work has always been about meeting people in the middle of change. For more than fifteen years, I have worked at the intersection of education and human development, believing that good learning design begins with deep respect for the learner.

Specializing in adult learning with technology, I approach curriculum design with both practice and principle—using frameworks like backward design and drawing on experiential learning theory.

02 — Philosophy

How I think
about learning

Meeting people where they are

Effective adult learning begins with the learner, not the curriculum. I ask who is in the room and what barriers might stand between them and the learning.

Equity as a design principle

Inclusive design isn't an add-on; it's foundational. My practice centres access, dignity, and belonging from the outset.
